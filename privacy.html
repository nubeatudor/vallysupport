2. Scope & Roles (Controller/Processor)

  • Website & Marketing (Controller). When you browse the Site, submit a form, or receive marketing communications, we act as a controller of your personal data.
  • Client Operations (Processor). When a business client engages us to provide the Services (e.g., supporting drivers and broker communications), we process personal data (e.g., driver names, contact numbers) as a processor on the client’s documented instructions. The DPA applies to that processing.

3. Data We Collect

3.1 Data you provide directly

  • Lead/Contact data: name, company, role, email, phone, fleet size, “main pain point,” message content, and consent preferences.
  • Client service data: driver and broker contact details, load identifiers, location and lane info, lumper/maintenance approvals, escalation contacts.
  • Communications: email and call content/metadata with our team (including recordings where legally permitted and disclosed).

3.2 Data collected automatically

  • Usage & device data: IP address, browser type/version, device identifiers, pages visited, time on page, referring/exit pages, and similar diagnostics.
  • Cookies/SDKs: essential cookies for site functionality; analytics/measurement cookies where enabled (see Section 11 and our Cookie Policy).

3.3 Data from third parties

  • Lead sources (e.g., referral partners), public business directories, or loadboard/maintenance vendors used by the Client in the course of Services.

4. How We Use Personal Data

  • To respond to enquiries and provide quotes, onboarding, account management, and the Services.
  • To coordinate with drivers, brokers, shippers, and service vendors as instructed by the Client.
  • To process payments and manage billing, fraud prevention, and collections.
  • To send service, security, or policy updates; and, with consent or as permitted by law, marketing communications you can opt out of at any time.
  • To maintain, secure, debug, and improve the Site and Services, including analytics.
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our agreements.

5. Legal Bases (GDPR)

Where the GDPR/UK GDPR applies, we rely on the following bases:

  • Consent (e.g., marketing emails/texts, non-essential cookies).
  • Contract (to perform the Services or respond to pre-contract requests).
  • Legitimate interests (to secure and improve the Site/Services, prevent abuse, or send limited B2B communications). We balance these interests against your rights.
  • Legal obligation (e.g., record-keeping, tax, responding to lawful requests).

6. Sharing & Disclosure

We share personal data only as necessary and with safeguards:

  • Vendors/Processors: hosting, email delivery, CRM, communications, analytics, and maintenance/fuel card vendors used during the Services. We require appropriate data-protection commitments.
  • Client-designated recipients: brokers, shippers, drivers, and other third parties with whom the Client instructs us to communicate.
  • Corporate transactions: in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to confidentiality.
  • Legal: to comply with law, protect rights, safety, or prevent fraud/abuse.

7. International Transfers

We operate from the Republic of Moldova and may process data in other countries. Where required, we use a lawful transfer mechanism (e.g., EU Standard Contractual Clauses) and implement appropriate safeguards.

8. Retention

  • Leads & enquiries: up to 24 months after last interaction.
  • Client service records (operational): typically 24 months, unless longer is needed for legal compliance or to establish/defend claims.
  • Billing & contracts: up to 6 years (or local legal requirement).
  • System logs: typically 12 months.
  • Marketing opt-out list: retained as needed to honor your preference.

9. Security

We apply administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the risk, including access controls, encrypted transport, least-privilege practices, vendor due diligence, and staff confidentiality obligations. No system is perfectly secure; please use caution when sharing data.

10. Your Privacy Rights

Your rights depend on your location’s laws. Subject to exemptions and verification, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectify inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Delete your data (erasure).
  • Restrict or object to certain processing (including direct marketing).
  • Portability of data you provided in a machine-readable format.
  • Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.
  • Appeal a decision on your request (certain U.S. states).

To make a request, email info@vallysupport.com. We will verify your identity and respond within applicable timelines (generally 30 days under GDPR and 45 days under U.S. state laws). If we process your data as a processor for a client, we will forward your request to the relevant client (controller).

You may also lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (e.g., an EU Data Protection Authority or the UK ICO).

11. Marketing, Cookies & Analytics

  • Marketing. We send B2B service updates and, with consent or as permitted by law, marketing communications. You can opt out anytime via the email footer or by contacting us.
  • Cookies. We use essential cookies and, where enabled, analytics/measurement cookies. See our Cookie Policy for categories, lifetimes, and how to manage preferences.
  • Signals. We honor browser-level global privacy controls where technically feasible.

12. Children’s Privacy

The Site and Services are directed to business users aged 18+. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13 (U.S.) or under 16 (EEA/UK). If you believe a child has provided data, contact us and we will take appropriate action.

13. “Do Not Sell/Share” (U.S. States)

We do not sell personal information for money. If we use analytics/advertising cookies that may constitute a “sale”/“share” under California or similar laws, you can opt out via our Cookie Preferences link. Authorized agents may submit requests by emailing info@vallysupport.com.

14. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy to reflect legal, technical, or business developments. We will post the revised Policy with an updated effective date and, where required, provide prior notice. Your continued use of the Site or Services after changes take effect signifies acceptance.
