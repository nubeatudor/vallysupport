1. What Are Cookies & Similar Technologies?

“Cookies” are small text files that a website stores on your device when you visit. They are widely used to make websites work, improve performance, and provide analytics. We also use related technologies such as local storage, pixels, and SDKs. For simplicity, we refer to all of these as “cookies.”

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We group cookies into the following categories. The table in Section 8 lists the specific cookies currently set.

2.1 Strictly Necessary

Required for the Site to function (e.g., load balancing, security, remembering your cookie choices). These cannot be switched off.

2.2 Functionality (Preferences)

Enable enhanced features such as remembering form inputs you’ve started, or your language/region preferences.

2.3 Performance & Analytics

Help us understand how visitors use the Site (e.g., page views, navigation flows) so we can improve content and UX. These run only if you consent (where required by law).

2.4 Advertising/Targeting

Used to deliver relevant advertising on other sites and measure campaign performance. We do not currently use advertising cookies on this Site. If we enable them in the future, you will be asked to provide consent.

3. Legal Basis for Cookies

Under EU/UK ePrivacy and GDPR, we rely on:

  • Legitimate interests for strictly necessary cookies (to provide a secure, functioning Site).
  • Consent for all non-essential cookies (e.g., analytics, advertising), which you can withdraw at any time.

Under certain U.S. state laws (e.g., California CPRA), some analytics/advertising cookies may be considered a “sale”/“share.” See Section 4.

4. How to Manage Your Preferences

On your first visit, you may see a cookie banner that allows you to accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can change your selection at any time:

  • Open Cookie Preferences (if a banner/manager is implemented), or
  • Use your browser controls (see Section 5).

We honor browser-level Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals where technically feasible by treating them as an opt-out for analytics/advertising cookies.

5. Browser Controls

You can set your browser to block or delete cookies. Instructions are typically found in browser help menus. Note that blocking strictly necessary cookies may break core Site functions (e.g., remembering consent).

6. Retention Periods

Cookies are either session (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent (stored until they expire or you delete them). Retention times for each cookie appear in the table below. We review these periodically to keep durations proportionate.

7. Third-Party Cookies & Transfers
